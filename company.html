About Super Micro Computer

Super Micro Computer, Inc. (Supermicro) is a Silicon Valley-based Total IT Solution Provider specializing in AI, Cloud, Storage, and 5G/Edge server infrastructure. Founded in 1993 by Charles Liang, the company designs, develops, manufactures, and sells energy-efficient, application-optimized server solutions based on x86 architecture.

The company went public on March 29, 2007 and has grown to become a top-5 global server vendor, with particular strength in GPU-accelerated AI server systems. Supermicro's key differentiator is its ability to rapidly bring new GPU platforms (NVIDIA, AMD, Intel) to market with both air-cooled and liquid-cooled configurations.

CEO & Founder

Charles Liang

President, CEO & Chairman of the Board

Born 1956 in Taiwan, Charles Liang is a Taiwanese-American electrical engineer and billionaire entrepreneur. He founded Supermicro in 1993 and has served as President, CEO, and Chairman since inception. He developed server system architecture expertise at Chips and Technologies (1987–1991) before founding Supermicro. He holds degrees from National Taiwan University and the University of Texas at Austin.

~12–15% insider ownership (with Sara Liu)

Sara Liu

Co-Founder & Senior Advisor

AI & HPC Servers

GPU & AI Server Systems

High-performance computing and AI training/inference servers optimized for data center workloads. Key series include Supermicro AI servers, HPC systems, and Hyperworks platforms. GPU server systems span the full range of NVIDIA DGX, HGX, and MGX platforms, as well as AMD Instinct-based systems and Blackwell-based systems (B200, B300, GB200 NVL72).

Storage

Enterprise Storage Solutions

Enterprise-grade storage systems including all-flash arrays, hybrid storage, and software-defined storage. Key series include Petascale JBOF, Flash systems, and NVMe solutions. Storage products are integrated with the company's Total IT Solutions approach.

Networking

High-Speed Networking

Networking infrastructure including switches, adapters, and interconnect solutions for high-speed data center networking. Solutions support 100G, 200G, and 400G switching speeds, integrated with the company's rack-scale IT solutions.

Rack-Level

Total IT Solutions

Full rack-scale IT solutions with integrated cooling, power, and management. The company's rack-level integrated systems combine servers, storage, and networking into turn-key AI factory solutions, particularly for hyperscaler and enterprise AI deployments.

Important: The following controversies involve serious governance, legal, and financial allegations. As of March 2026, some matters remain unresolved. This section is provided for research completeness.
Critical

Ernst & Young Resignation

Oct 24, 2024

EY resigned as Supermicro's auditor on October 24, 2024, after raising significant concerns about the company's internal controls, board independence, and accounting practices. Shares dropped approximately 33% on the news. EY's resignation came two months after Hindenburg Research published a critical short report.

Note: Supermicro disagreed with EY's decision and does not expect matters raised by EY to result in restatement of quarterly reports for FY2024 or prior years.

High Severity

Hindenburg Research Allegations

Aug 27, 2024

Hindenburg Research (now defunct) published a detailed short report alleging "glaring accounting flags, evidence of undisclosed related party transactions, and fresh evidence of accounting manipulation" at Supermicro. The report claimed Supermicro had engaged in improper revenue recognition and circumvented internal accounting controls. Note: Hindenburg shut down operations in 2024 after its Supermicro report.

High Severity

DOJ & SEC Subpoenas

Sep 26, 2024

The Department of Justice (DOJ) and Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) subpoenaed Supermicro for documents following the publication of Hindenburg's allegations. The company disclosed these requests in a February 2025 filing. As of March 2026, the status of these inquiries remains unresolved.

Moderate

Nasdaq Non-Compliance

Sep 2024

Supermicro received a Nasdaq non-compliance notification in September 2024 due to delayed 10-K and 10-Q filings. The company submitted a compliance plan on November 18, 2024, and regained compliance in February 2025 after filing outstanding reports.

Moderate

Accounting Leadership Changes

2024–2025

Following EY's resignation, Supermicro underwent significant accounting leadership changes as part of its efforts to address internal control concerns and restore confidence with investors and the new auditor BDO USA.
